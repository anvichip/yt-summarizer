The iPhone 15 series includes four new models: iPhone 15, iPhone 15 Plus, iPhone 15 Pro, and iPhone 15 Pro Max. All four models now have USB Type-C. The display has smaller bezels and a dynamic island, replacing the notch. The display is brighter but lacks promotion technology, limiting it to 60 hertz. The build has a contoured edge and new color options with a matte finish. The primary camera has a 48 megapixel sensor for better low light images and offers two times lossless zoom. The new chip, A16 Bionic, improves efficiency and battery life. The iPhone 15 Plus has the best battery life of the bunch. The pricing for iPhone 15 and 15 Plus starts at $800 and $900, respectively. The iPhone 15 Pro and Pro Max feature titanium bodies, making them more durable and lighter. The display has promotion technology with 120 hertz and slightly smaller bezels. The A17 Pro chip offers improved GPU and CPU performance, as well as better ray tracing capabilities. The Pro models have a customizable action button and new camera features like three times and five times optical zoom. The iPhone 15 Pro Max has up to 25 times digital zoom. The Pro models also support spatial video and ProRes video recording. The iPhone 15 Pro and Pro Max have USB 3, while the iPhone 15 and 15 Plus have USB 2. The pricing for iPhone 15 Pro starts at $1,000 and $1,200 for the Pro Max, both in the 256 GB model. Pre-orders start on Friday, and the phones will be available on September 22nd.
